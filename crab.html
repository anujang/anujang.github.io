Singapore Chilli Crab

Author: Barney Desmazery

Credit: BBC Good Foods

 Give a rating

Prep: 25 mins

Cook: 5 mins

Difficulty: Medium

Servings: 2

This low fat, spicy crab dish is a versatile street-food style recipe that can be adapted to your liking. Serve with rice or steamed bao buns.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole cooked crab (about 1kg)
  • 2 tbsp flavourless oil
  • 3 garlic cloves , very finely chopped
  • thumb-sized piece ginger , very finely chopped
  • 3 red chillies , 2 very finely chopped, 1 sliced
  • 4 tbsp tomato ketchup
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • handful coriander leaves, roughly chopped
  • 2 spring onions , sliced
  • rice or steamed bad buns, to serve

Recipe:

  1. The crab must be prepared before stir-frying (you can ask your fishmonger to do this).
  2. This involves removing the claws, the main shell, discarding the dead manâ€™s fingers, then cutting the body into four pieces, and cracking the claws and the legs so the sauce can get through to the meat.
  3. Heat the oil in a large wok and sizzle the garlic, ginger and chopped chillies for 1 min or until fragrant.
  4. Add the ketchup, soy and 100ml water, and stir to combine. Throw in the crab, turn up the heat and stir-fry for 3-5 mins or until the crab is piping hot and coated in the sauce.
  5. < Stir through most of the coriander, spring onions and sliced chilli.
  6. Use tongs to arrange the crab on a serving dish, pour over the sauce from the pan and scatter over the remaining coriander, spring onions and sliced chilli.
  7. Serve with rice or bao buns, and a lot of napkins.
