Griddled halloumi with watermelon & caper breadcrumbs

Author: Diana Henry

Credit: BBC Good Foods

 Give a rating

Prep: 30 mins

Cook: 15 mins

Difficulty: Easy

Servings: 4

This salad has a great interplay of textures, temperatures and flavours. Toss juicy watermelon with salty cheese, crispy breadcrumbs, capers, almonds and mint.

Ingredients:

  • 750g watermelon
  • 2 small shallots , very finely sliced
  • 100ml extra virgin olive oil , plus extra for brushing and to serve
  • 4 tsp white balsamic vinegar
  • 300g halloumi , sliced
  • good squeeze of lemon juice

Recipe:

  1. Cut the watermelon into either cubes or slices and transfer to a bowl with the shallots.
  2. Add 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil and the balsamic, then season with fresh black pepper.
  3. Cover and put in the fridge for about 15-20 mins.
  4. Heat the remaining oil in a frying pan. Fry the breadcrumbs until turning gold.
  5. Add the garlic, chilli and capers and cook for another minute, then toss in the almonds.
  6. Remove from the heat, add the torn mint (it will wilt a little), then taste – it will be quite salty, but the watermelon won’t be, so you’ll get a good contrast.
  7. Brush the halloumi with a little oil and heat a griddle pan.
  8. Griddle the cheese on both sides until just starting to melt but still holding its shape, with nice golden griddle marks.
  9. Toss the cold watermelon and shallots with the rest of the mint leaves and some salt. Divide between four plates, or pile onto a platter.
  10. Put the hot halloumi on top. Squeeze over some lemon juice and top with some of the breadcrumb and caper mix. Serve immediately.
